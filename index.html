Andres Iniesta

Iniesta playing a match for FC Barcelona


Albacete

  • Andres Iniesta was born in a small village called Fuentealbilla, in the province of Albacete, Castile–La Mancha, Spain. It was in this club where Iniesta spent the very early years of his youth career as a footballer. During Iniesta's stay at Albacete, he attracted the attention of scouts from clubs in Spain, and his parents decided to travel to Barcelona to enroll him in the youth ranks.


FC Barcelona

  • At age 12, Iniesta joined FC Barcelona's youth academy - "La Masia." He excelled as a youth player, and was team captain of the U-15 team that won the Nike Premier Cup in 1999. He scored a last minute goal in the final, which gave Barcelona the victory, and was also named player of the tournament.

  • Iniesta played with Barcelona's B team for a short time, but played his first official match with FC Barcelona in October 2002. It was at Barcelona where Iniesta spent the major part of his career as a professional footballer. His role was vital as a playmaker in the seasons to come, and he developed into a more mature footballer. As Iniesta developed, he became better and better, and soon evolved into an indispensable player for FC Barcelona. In the pre-season of 2007, he helped the team win the Joan Gamper Trophy, and scored important goals in the league games, and Champions League.

  • In 2008, Iniesta was elected fourth-captain of the team, cementing him as one of the most important squad members and one of the leaders. In 2009 during a semi-final match against Chelsea FC, he scored a goal in the final minute of extra time, that helped Barcelona go through to the final. Later that year, he was 4th place for the Ballon d'Or, and 5th place for the 2009 FIFA World Player of the Year, making him among the top 5 footballers on the planet.

  • During the 2009-2010 season, Iniesta helped Barcelona achieve 99 points - a record number. This secured the league title for them that season. Iniesta went on to win the 2010-2011 La Liga season again with Barcelona, scoring important goals and making important plays. More and more trophies followed the following years, with Iniesta being named as captain of the club after Xavi's departure. He helped Barcelona win the Champions League, UEFA Super Cup, and FIFA Club World Cup in 2015. He signed a lifetime contract with Barcelona, but evenually left the club in 2018, making him one of the most important players in the history of the club.


Spain National Team

  • Andres Iniesta has also been called up to play for the Spanish National Team, and has been one of their more important figures in the past years. His first official match with Spain was in 2006, during a friendly match against Russia. He was then called up for international duty to participate in the World Cup that year.

  • Since his first call up for Spain, Iniesta became one of Spain's most important players. He was crucial to the team when they won the UEFA Euro Cup in 2008, and in 2010, Iniesta achieved world glory when he helped Spain reach the FIFA World Cup final, and gave them the victory in the 116th minute of the match. Iniesta was also a key figure in 2012 for Spain, helping them win the UEFA Euro Cup for the second time in a row.

  • In 2013, Iniesta helped Spain reach the final of the 2013 Confederations Cup, where they eventually lost to the host nation, Brazil. He was awarded the Silver Ball award, for being the second best player of the tournament. At the UEFA Euro 2016, Spain did not perform as well as they had before and were knocked out, and a smiliar scenario happened at the 2018 World Cup, where Spain was eliminated in penalty kicks. It was here where Iniesta decided to retire from international football.
